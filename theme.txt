Theme 1: Next-gen control system interface

Problem overview
This project invites young innovators to rethink how small-scale industrial control systems should look, feel, and operate in the future. With increasing system complexity and a growing volume of alarms and notifications, operators often struggle to identify what truly matters. This challenge provides an opportunity to explore modern, intuitive, and intelligent UX approaches using platforms such as Raspberry Pi, Arduino, or software simulators.

Problem statement
Traditional control system interfaces (HMIs) generate a continuous stream of alerts. Operators must manually filter and interpret them, which can lead to missed critical alarms, slower response times, and operational inefficiencies. Existing HMIs often lack personalization, modern interaction patterns, and intelligent prioritization.

Stakeholders

    Control system operators who need clear, actionable information
    Engineers and system designers responsible for configuring and maintaining HMIs
    Industrial organizations that rely on safe and efficient operations
    Students and UX designers exploring innovative interaction models

Current workarounds
Today, operators rely on:

    Basic alarm lists or color-coded indicators
    Manual filtering or sorting of notifications
    Standard HMI screens with limited customization
    Rule-based alarm grouping that often requires heavy manual setup

​These approaches are functional but not adaptive, intelligent, or user-centric.

Desired solution

The ideal next-generation control interface incorporates:

    Auto-generated HMI screens to reduce engineering time
    Signal templates and reusable libraries for standardization
    Model-based or metadata-driven engineering for efficiency and consistency
    AI-assisted configuration to simplify setup and adapt to context
    Low-code/no-code tools, so non-experts can modify interfaces
    Role-based, context-aware UIs that highlight the right information to the right users
    Modern UX patterns inspired by popular apps such as Google Maps, Microsoft Teams, or streaming services, prioritizing simplicity, clarity, and adaptability

Impact and benefits
A redesigned control system interface can produce measurable benefits:

    Faster operator decision-making
    Reduced alarm fatigue
    Improved safety and system awareness
    Lower engineering and configuration effort
    Higher quality and consistency of interface designs
